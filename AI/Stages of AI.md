# Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI)

Also known as weak or narrow AI, Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI) is the most successfully realised form of artificial intelligence. It is specifically goal-oriented and is designed to carry out a single task extremely well. ANI uses techniques such as natural language processing (NLP), deep learning, and machine learning to perform these tasks. All expert systems that emulate the decision making ability of humans belong to this category.

Rankbrain by Google, which is a component of Google Search, uses ANI to get relevant search results. Speech recognition/voice assistant systems such as Siri by Apple and Alexa by Amazon, are other common examples of ANI.


# Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

AGI, also called strong or deep AI, is a more advanced form of artificial intelligence than ANI. An AGI-powered machine can mimic human intelligence and can learn and apple the same in teal time situations or problems. The function of AGI goes beyond executing singular goal-oriented tasks to perform proactive actions.

For instance, an autonomous or driverless car uses multiple sensor systems and control functions simultaneously to successfully drive and avoid accidents.

# Artificial Super Intelligence (ASI)

ASI is far beyond mimicking or replicating human intelligence. In its current conception, it is hypothetical and is the most advanced form of AI. An ASI-powered machine would be self-aware and could exceed the capacity of human intelligence across a range of areas.

This conception of super-intelligence has been a part of science fiction for a long time. However, it is debatable whether such forms of AI could be helpful or could pose a threat to humans.
