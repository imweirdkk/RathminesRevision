# Definition of Artificial Intelligence (AI)
"Artificial Intelligence" is a branch of computer science dealing with computers or machines that imitate human intelligence.

The goal of these computers or machines is to perform various tasks commonly associated with intelligent beings.

# Difference between Traditional Technology and AI
Traditional technology is incapable of predicting human choices, such as emotions, and struggled to carry out any higher-order processing that creates outputs from a 'relative' input. It simply followed commands and executes them.
## AI can predict

An AI-based program is able to predict patters in human thought and is capable of presenting solutions based on relative or imprecise data. Unlike traditional machines that can only see black and white, AI works in gray area.

# One way to check whether a system is artificially intelligent is to run it through the "Turing Test"

The Turing Test, designed by Alan Turing in the 1950, tries to answer if machines can mimic human intelligence

In the test, there are three participants in three separate rooms - an interrogator, a human responder and a computer.

The interrogator quizzes the two participants - the human responder and the AI computer - on a range of topics. All responses from the participants are recorded. 

After a set time or set number of questions, the interrogator has to identify which responses were from the computer and which ones were from the human participant. 

This test is repeated multiple times and if in the process, the interrogator makes the wrong identification in more than half the responses, the computer is considered to have artificial intelligence.

This AI test is interesting not so much from a technical point of view, but from a philosophical one - it introduces the idea that machines could potentially reach a level of human like intelligence, which was a revolutionary concept in the mid 20th century. ****
