![[Star Topology.jpg]]

**Positives**:
- Easy to build, manage and expand
- A failed cable affects only one devices letting the network stay up
- High performance with switches

**Negatives**:
- The centre device is critical, if that goes down the entire network does too, fix with redundancies
- Uses more cabling than a [[Bus Topology|bus]]

**Use cases**:
- Modern Ethernet LANs 
- Wi-Fi cells (each AP is the centre for its clients).

**Extra notes**:
- The central switch is a single point of failure; use redundant core/distribution switches to avoid outages
