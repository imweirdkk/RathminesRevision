**Definition:**
- A CAN spans multiple interconnected buildings within a specific geographical area, larder than a LAN but smaller than a MAN/WAN

**Purpose:**
- Interconnects multiple independent LANs so they can share central resources, databases, or a single internet gateway

**Technologies:**
- High-speed fibre optic backbones
- Core routing switches
- Extended wireless bridges

**Challenges:**
- High initial infrastructure costs (e.g., trenching cables between cables)
- Securing a large physical perimeter
