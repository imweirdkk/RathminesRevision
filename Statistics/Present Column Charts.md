- Column charts use vertical bars to represent data
- Each column represents a different category of data and their heights represents the frequency of that data
- Bar should usually be separated by a gap
- Bars should usually start at 0

- The frequency table below shows the number of text messages received by a group of teenagers on a Saturday

| Number of messages  |  0  |  1  |  2  |  3  |  4  |  5  |  6  |  7  |  8  |
| :-----------------: | :-: | :-: | :-: | :-: | :-: | :-: | :-: | :-: | :-: |
| Number of teenagers |  2  |  5  |  9  |  7  |  3  |  8  |  6  |  5  |  4  |
![[statsBarChart.png]]

**Ensure that you label your axes and that there is a gap between each column**


- Column charts can represent multiple data sets on one chart
- In this case, the different data sets should be grouped together within each category

| Quarter | Kent  | Lincoln | Mersey | York  |
| ------- | ----- | ------- | ------ | ----- |
| Q1      | 44700 | 52800   | 43500  | 38800 |
| Q2      | 45000 | 36500   | 41000  | 34100 |
| Q3      | 51200 | 44200   | 39700  | 27000 |
| Q4      | 56500 | 45300   | 41200  | 48900 |
- Columns should be separated by one category but joined by another
- In this case, columns are grouped by locations but separated by quarter, you could also group by quarter and separated by location
