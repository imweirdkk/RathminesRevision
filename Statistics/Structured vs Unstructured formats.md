
**Structured** question formats have prepared answers that we present to respondents. Typically used to collect [[Quantitative - Numerical Data|quantitative]] data.

**Unstructured** question formats leave the answer space open for respondents to give their answers in whatever way they like. Typically used to collect [[Qualitative - Categorical data|qualitative]] data

The main type of structured question formats will be:
- [[Dichotomous]]
- [[Likert scale]]
- [[Semantic differential scale]] 

## Outlining unstructured question formats
- Unstructured question formats are open-ended, we don't prescribe any particular style, format or possible answers.
- These may be standalone questions where we ask for comments, descriptions, explanations, etc...
- Often they are follow-ups to structured questions, e.g.
	- Have you made an appointment with the guidance counsellor this year? Yes/No ([[Dichotomous]])
	- If no, why? (**unstructured**)
- We may allow any number for age, frequency etc in an answer box rather than choosing from a list.

## Outline appropriate uses for each format
- *Structured question* formats typically give [[Quantitative - Numerical Data|quantitative]] data (or data that can be easily altered to quantitative) so are better suited for questionnaires. 
	- High response rate - they are easy and quick to answer so we tend to get lots of answers
	- Clarity and accuracy - they are usually simple and giving potential answers helps respondents understand the question even better
	- Consistency - All answers should be from a list of possibilities, so they are easy to compare
	- Relevancy - by only allowing specific answers, we make sure they are the kind of answers we are looking for
- *Unstructured question* formats typically give [[Qualitative - Categorical data|qualitative]]  data so are better suited for reviews
	- Flexibility - no specific answer format means respondents can give any answers they deem appropriate
	- Understanding - answers can be more detailed, allowing for deeper insights
	- Context - following on from structured questions allows respondents to explain their answers
	- Completeness - allowing respondents to give any answers also means that we are not limited by the questions we think to ask
