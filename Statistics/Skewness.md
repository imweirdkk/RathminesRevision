- Skewness is a measure of how asymmetric a data set is
![[Skewed left or Negative.png]]
This has a negative skew or skewed left
![[Skewed right or Positive Skew.png]]

- Data with low skewness is symmetric
![[lowSkewness.png]]

- A positive skew will have the mode at the peak; median further down the right and lastly the mean
![[positiveSkewness.png]]
- A negative skew will also have the mode as the peak, median further down to the left and lastly the mean
![[negativeSkewness.png]]

- A symmetrical distribution, the mean, median and mode will all be at the peak
![[symmetricalDistribution.png]]

- In particular we must study the normal distribution:
	- It has a characteristic bell-shape
	- It is symmetrical (and so the mean, median and mode all lie in the centre)
	- It has a single peak in the centre and falls off towards the sides
	- We can discuss proportions of the data in parts of the distribution e.g. ~65% of the data lies within $\sigma$ or ~97.7% of the data is $2\sigma$ above the mean or less (using z-scores)
